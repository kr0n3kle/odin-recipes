Smoked Beef Brisket

Description

The classic low and slow smoked beef brisket that everyone loves! Done correctly, this requires no sauce!

Ingredients

  • 1 8-22lb full size packer brisket (prime grade or higher preferred)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic powder

Steps

  • Start fire in pit and stablize temperature between 225F to 240F degrees, water pan filled if equipped
  • Trim brisket fat cap to approximately 1/4" thickness. Remove excess fat around point side also.
  • Coat brisket liberally with mixture of salt, pepper and garlic. Do not underuse seasoning!
  • Important: Place brisket into smoker with fat cap facing towards the direction of the heat source.
  • Spray very lightly with water or other liquid of your choice once every hour or two up to 5 hours.
  • After about 5 hours, remove the brisket and wrap tightly in butcher paper, noting the direction the fat cap faces.
  • Place brisket back into smoker like before. Continue to cook until the brisket is jiggly which occurs around 200F IT.

Some important notes

Do not continue to check on the brisket while it cooks. If you lookin' you ain't cookin'! Do not overspray the brisket, you will lose seasoning!
